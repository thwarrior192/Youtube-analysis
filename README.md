# YouTube Channel Analysis

## Project Overview

This project explores and analyzes a dataset of popular YouTube channels to uncover trends in subscribers, video views, content categories, and channel performance. The goal is to understand what factors contribute to a channel's success and identify patterns among top-performing YouTube creators.

---

## Objectives

The main objectives of this project are:

* Identify the most subscribed YouTube channels.
* Identify the most viewed YouTube channels.
* Analyze the distribution of YouTube channel categories.
* Explore the relationship between subscribers and video views.
* Examine the relationship between video count and video views.
* Discover correlations between different channel metrics.
* Compare average subscribers across content categories.

---

## Dataset Information

The dataset contains information about popular YouTube channels, including:

* Rank
* Channel Name (Youtuber)
* Subscribers
* Video Views
* Video Count
* Category
* Year Started

---

## Data Cleaning

The following data cleaning steps were performed:

* Loaded the dataset using Pandas.
* Checked dataset structure and data types.
* Identified and handled missing values.
* Checked for duplicate records.
* Converted numerical columns into appropriate formats.
* Prepared the dataset for visualization and analysis.

---

## Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

### 1. Top 10 Most Subscribed YouTubers

This visualization highlights the channels with the highest subscriber counts.

**Key Insight:**

* A small number of channels dominate YouTube's subscriber base.
* Entertainment and music-related channels are among the most subscribed creators.

---

### 2. Top 10 Most Viewed Channels

This visualization shows the channels that have accumulated the highest number of views.

**Key Insight:**

* High view counts are concentrated among a few major channels.
* Subscriber count alone does not fully determine total views.

---

### 3. Distribution of YouTube Channels by Category

This analysis explores the frequency of channels across different content categories.

**Key Insight:**

* Certain categories have a significantly larger presence on the platform.
* Entertainment, Music, and Gaming categories appear frequently among top channels.

---

### 4. Subscribers vs Video Views

This scatter plot examines the relationship between subscriber count and total video views.

**Key Insight:**

* Channels with more subscribers generally receive more views.
* A positive relationship exists between audience size and content reach.

---

### 5. Video Count vs Video Views

This visualization explores whether uploading more videos leads to higher view counts.

**Key Insight:**

* Uploading more content does not always guarantee more views.
* Content quality and audience engagement play an important role.

---

### 6. Correlation Heatmap

A correlation heatmap was created to understand relationships between numerical variables.

**Key Insight:**

* Subscribers and video views show strong positive correlation.
* Some metrics are more influential than others in determining channel success.

---

### 7. Average Subscribers by Category

This analysis compares the average subscriber counts across different content categories.

**Key Insight:**

* Some categories consistently attract larger audiences.
* Content niche has a significant impact on channel growth.

---

## Key Findings

* Subscriber count and video views are strongly related.
* Popular channels dominate a large share of YouTube's audience.
* Different categories attract different audience sizes.
* High video output does not necessarily translate into higher viewership.
* Content category influences subscriber growth and channel performance.

---

## Conclusion

This project provides valuable insights into the YouTube ecosystem by analyzing channel performance, audience reach, and content categories. The findings demonstrate that while subscriber count and video views are closely connected, factors such as content category and engagement also play an important role in determining channel success.

Through data cleaning, visualization, and exploratory analysis, this project showcases practical data analysis skills using Python, Pandas, Matplotlib, and Seaborn.
